Write Up

"Zero" by the Smashing Pumpkins and J.D. Salinger's novel "The Catcher in the Rye" are both
portrayals of the struggle of alienation, the yearning for connection and the pain of
disillusionment. While the two pieces of media are aesthetically very different their themes
resonate to an incredible degree. "My reflection, dirty mirror / There's no connection to myself /
I'm your lover, I'm your zero / I'm the face in your dreams of glass." These lyrics mirror the
general themes of isolation and alienation that hold exemplifies. The image of Holden, standing
before a large mirror, staring at an alternative version of himself, someone who is socially adept
and does not struggle with being so isolated from society. Yet, his figure in front of the mirror,
accentuates his reality, alone and desolate. The disconnect between what he wants and reality
emphasizes the gap between what he wants for his life and what he has experienced. The singer
says "She's the one for me / She's all I really need", these lyrics echo the unfulfilled connection
that characterizes Holden through the novel, him being in love with Jane Gallagher. The image
of the tennis racket and the checkerboard are physical representations of Jane in Holden's life.
The lyrics "Emptiness is loneliness, and loneliness is cleanliness / And cleanliness is godliness,
and God is empty / Just like me" resonate with Holden's Disillusionment with the adult world.
The image of Holden standing alone in a white barren room contrasted by a colorful outside
world represents the contrast between what he perceives as a fake, phony adult world which he
inhabits, versus, the world of childhood that he desires to return to and protect.
